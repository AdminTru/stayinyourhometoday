Understanding Utah's Foreclosure Process

Knowledge is Power—Here's What You Need to Know

Foreclosure in Utah follows a specific timeline. Understanding where you are in the process helps you know what actions you can still take.

📋 PHASE 1: Notice of Default (After 3 Missed Payments)

What It Means: Your lender has filed a Notice of Default with the county because you're behind on payments. This is the first formal step in the foreclosure process.

What You Can Still Do:

  • Work with your lender on a loan modification or repayment plan
  • Apply for government assistance programs (if eligible)
  • Explore refinancing options (if you have equity)
  • File for bankruptcy to pause the foreclosure temporarily
  • Pursue a short sale if staying isn't viable
  • List your home for sale and try to sell before auction

⚠️ PHASE 2: Auction Scheduled

What It Means: Your lender has scheduled a public auction sale. This is the final phase before you lose ownership of your home.

What You Can Still Do:

  • Pay off the full amount owed to stop the sale (reinstate the loan)
  • File for bankruptcy to immediately halt the auction
  • Negotiate a last-minute settlement with your lender
  • Explore a quick cash sale if time permits
  • Contact a foreclosure attorney for emergency legal options

🔨 PHASE 3: Auction Completed

What It Means: Your home has been sold at auction. Ownership transfers to the buyer (often the lender). You have limited time before eviction proceedings begin.

Timeline: Eviction can occur within days to weeks after the auction.

What You Can Still Do:

  • Negotiate "cash for keys" with the new owner
  • Arrange a dignified move-out timeline
  • Consult with a bankruptcy attorney about post-auction options
  • Focus on credit repair and future housing plans

🔑 The Bottom Line: While options are severely limited after auction, we can still help you transition with dignity and plan your next steps.

Your Options Side-By-Side

Let's Look at What's Realistically Available to You

💰 Loan Modification

Best For:

Homeowners who want to stay and can afford a modified payment

How It Works:

Your lender permanently changes your loan terms—potentially lowering your interest rate, extending the loan term, or adding missed payments to the balance.

Timeline:

Can take 30-90 days. Requires proof of hardship and financial documents.

🏡 Short Sale

Best For:

Homeowners who owe more than the home is worth and need to move on

How It Works:

Your lender agrees to accept less than what you owe. You sell your home on the open market, avoid foreclosure, and potentially walk away with no deficiency.

Timeline:

Typically 3-6 months. Requires lender approval and active marketing.

📊 Traditional Sale

Best For:

Homeowners with equity who have time to market properly

How It Works:

List your home on the MLS, maximize exposure, get top dollar through traditional marketing, and pay off your loan with the proceeds.

Timeline:

Typically 30-90 days. Requires at least 45+ days before auction.

⚡ Quick Cash Sale

Best For:

Homeowners who need to close fast and don't want showings or repairs

How It Works:

Sell to a trusted cash buyer (often at a discount) in exchange for speed, certainty, and zero hassle. No repairs, no staging, no uncertainty.

Timeline:

As fast as 7-14 days. Ideal when auction is approaching quickly.

⚖️ Bankruptcy Protection

Best For:

Homeowners who need immediate protection and time to reorganize finances

How It Works:

Filing bankruptcy triggers an automatic stay that immediately stops foreclosure. Chapter 13 allows you to catch up over 3-5 years. Chapter 7 buys temporary time.

Timeline:

Can stop auction within 24 hours of filing. Long-term consequences.

❌ Do Nothing

Reality Check:

If you do nothing, here's exactly what happens

What Happens:

Your home sells at auction. You lose any equity. Foreclosure destroys your credit for 7 years. You may still owe a deficiency. Eviction follows quickly.

Timeline:

Auction proceeds as scheduled. Eviction within weeks afterward.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to be in foreclosure to get help?

No. We work with homeowners who are behind on payments but haven't received a Notice of Default yet, as well as those further into the foreclosure process.

How much does your consultation cost?

Nothing. Our initial consultation and options review are completely free. We only get paid if you choose to work with us on a short sale or cash offer.

How long does the process take?

It depends on which option you pursue. A loan modification can take 2-3 months. A short sale typically takes 3-6 months. A cash offer can close in as little as 2-3 weeks. We'll give you realistic timelines during your options review.

What if I'm too embarrassed to reach out?

We get it. But please know that there's nothing to be ashamed of. Life happens—job loss, medical bills, divorce, unexpected expenses. We've seen it all, and we're here to help, not judge. The sooner you reach out, the more options you typically have.

What if I already received a Notice of Default?

You still have time. In Utah, there's a minimum three-month period between the Notice of Default and the foreclosure sale. Don't wait until the last minute, but know that it's not too late.

Will working with you hurt my credit?

Your credit is already being affected if you're behind on payments. The question is which option will help you minimize additional damage. We'll be honest about the credit impact of each choice.

Do I have to move immediately after selling to avoid foreclosure?

No. We offer flexible closing dates and post-closing occupancy agreements. You can stay 30-60 days after closing if needed.

What happens if foreclosure goes to auction?

Property sells to highest bidder. Any excess after mortgage payoff goes to you. Deficiency balance may still be owed. Credit severely damaged for 7 years.
